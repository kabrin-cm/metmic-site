Before Your Call

4 Things to Have Ready

Your call is 15 minutes. These four items help Suki make every minute count for you.

1

Your Investment Amount

A rough idea of how much you would like to invest. The minimum is $25,000, but many investors start with more. Just have a ballpark number in mind.

2

Your Account Type

Know which accounts you are working with. RRSP, TFSA, RRIF, RESP, FHSA, LIRA, LIF, or non-registered. This helps Suki show you the tax implications that matter most.

3

Your Financial Goal

Are you looking for monthly income you can spend? Tax-sheltered growth inside a registered account? A better return on corporate retained earnings? Each path looks a little different.

4

Your Questions

Write down anything you want to ask. About safety, returns, liquidity, regulation, fees. There are no wrong questions. The more prepared you are, the more valuable those 15 minutes become.

On the Call

What Happens During Your 15 Minutes

Minutes 1-3: Your Situation

Suki asks about where your money is now, what accounts you are using, and what kind of income or growth you are looking for. No scripts. A real conversation.

Minutes 4-8: Your Numbers

Based on your situation, Suki explains how Metmic works specifically for someone in your position. Not a generic overview. Real numbers showing what your investment could look like.

Minutes 9-12: Your Questions

This is your time. Ask anything about safety, returns, liquidity, regulation, fees. Suki is a CPA. He gives direct answers, not sales pitches.

Minutes 13-15: Next Steps

If Metmic makes sense for your situation, Suki outlines what happens next. If it does not, he will tell you that directly. He has spent 30 years in accounting and finance. He does not recommend things that do not work for the person sitting across from him.
