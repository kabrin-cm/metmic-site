There's a Better Way to Invest in Real Estate. Most Canadians Just Don't Know About It.

A Mortgage Investment Corporation, or MIC, is one of Canada's best-kept investment secrets.

The concept is straightforward. A MIC pools capital from investors like you and lends it out as mortgages secured by real estate. Borrowers pay interest on those mortgages. That interest flows back to you as income. Every dollar of lending is backed by a physical property in British Columbia.

You get real estate returns. Without the tenants, the toilets, or the 3 AM phone calls.

MICs have existed in Canada since the 1970s. They're recognized by the CRA. Regulated by securities commissions. Required to distribute 100% of net income to shareholders. And they're eligible for registered accounts.

Your Questions, Answered

Is my money safe? What happens if the real estate market drops?
Every mortgage in Metmic's portfolio is secured by physical BC real estate with conservative loan-to-value ratios. The property backing your investment is worth more than the loan amount. In 13 years of operation, including the 2020 market disruption and the 2022 rate hikes, Metmic has delivered consistent returns. The portfolio is diversified across 73 mortgages with no single borrower exceeding 15% of total assets.
What exactly is a MIC? Is this legitimate?
Mortgage Investment Corporations have been a recognized investment structure in Canada since the 1970s. They're established under the Income Tax Act, regulated by securities commissions, and required to distribute 100% of net income to shareholders. Metmic holds a BBB A+ rating, is registered with the BC Securities Commission, and undergoes annual independent audits.
How does this compare to GICs?
GICs currently pay around 4-4.5%. Metmic's 10-year average return is 8.45%. On a $100,000 investment, that's the difference between earning $4,000 per year and $8,450 per year. Over 10 years, that gap adds up to $44,500 in additional income. GICs are CDIC-insured, which is a genuine advantage. But Metmic's mortgages are backed by real property, the portfolio is diversified, and the returns have been consistent across multiple market conditions.
Can I hold this in my TFSA or RRSP?
Yes. Metmic shares are eligible for TFSA, RRSP, RESP, FHSA, LIRA, LIRSP, and RRIF accounts. This means your 8%+ returns can compound tax-free or tax-deferred, which significantly increases your effective return compared to holding the same investment in a non-registered account.
What's the minimum investment?
$5,000. Most investors start with more, but the entry point is accessible.
Can I get my money out when I need it?
MIC shares are redeemable, and Metmic has a history of honoring redemptions throughout its 13 years of operation. That said, this isn't a savings account. There may be notice periods for redemptions. The discovery call is a good place to discuss liquidity needs specific to your situation.
Who will I speak with on the call?
A co-founder. Suki Gill (CPA, CGA) or a dealing representative from the Metropointe Capital team. These are the people who actually manage the portfolio. Not a sales team reading from a script.
I'm not in BC. Can I still invest?
Yes. BC residents invest through Metropointe Capital Inc. (the registered Exempt Market Dealer). Investors in other provinces can access Metmic shares through Parvis Investment Services. We'll walk you through the right path on the discovery call.
