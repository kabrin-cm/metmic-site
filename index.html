The Problem

Your "Safe" GIC Is Quietly Falling Behind

Right now, the best 5-year GIC in Canada pays 3.85%. A 1-year GIC pays 3.40%. Meanwhile, Canadian inflation sits at 2.7%. After taxes and inflation, your real return on a GIC is close to zero.

  • Your GIC pays 3.5%. Inflation takes 2.7%. Taxes take another chunk. You are left with pennies of real purchasing power growth.
  • GIC rates are falling. In 2023, you could lock in above 5%. Today, every renewal notice is lower than the last.
  • Your RRIF is shrinking faster than your GIC can fill it. At age 72, the government requires you to withdraw a minimum of 5.28% from your RRIF. If your portfolio earns 3.5%, you are drawing down principal every year. By 80, the minimum jumps to 6.82%.
  • The real risk is doing nothing. At 3.5%, $300,000 generates $10,500 per year. That is $875 a month before taxes. For a 30-year retirement, that is not a plan.
